# Resume and CV Building, Values Exercises, and Networking

* [Drafting a Personal Mission Statement](assets/pdfs/personal_statement.pdf)
This exercise from the Nonprofit Academy of Wisconsin guides you through creating a mission statement you can use on a resume and to shape how you pitch yourself in an interview. It draws on personal anecdotes and associated values/causes.
* [The Informational Interview](https://www.insidehighered.com/blogs/gradhacker/informational-interview) and [The Importance of Informational Interviews](https://www.insidehighered.com/advice/2020/06/01/given-uncertainty-about-faculty-hiring-fall-job-seekers-should-actively-seek?utm_source=sfmc&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=great&utm_content=newsletter)
These articles define informational interviews and their value for graduate students on the job market.
* [When, How and Why to Conduct Informational Interviews](https://www.insidehighered.com/advice/2021/01/28/advice-making-most-informational-interviews-opinion)
This article explains the value of informational interviews and building meaningful connections as a regular and ongoing part of career and professional development.
